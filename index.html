FAQs
Multiple hackathon wins - what drives me as a builder?

Honestly? I love the thrill of building things from zero to something real. Winning multiple hackathons internationally wasn't just about the prize money – it was about proving I could solve complex problems under pressure and walk away with bragging rights. What really gets me excited is taking an idea, coding it into existence, and watching people actually use it. Whether it's Trimmly making it easier to book a haircut or building analytics dashboards that make clients go "whoa, I can actually understand my data now" – I'm hooked on creating tools that just work.

Computer Science graduate with a 3.95 CGPA - am I a robot or do I have a life?

Plot twist: I'm definitely not a robot, though my coffee consumption might suggest otherwise. That 3.95 CGPA from UPM came from being obsessively curious and weirdly competitive about understanding how things work. But here's the thing – while I was grinding through algorithms and data structures, I was also winning hackathons internationally, building Trimmly, and proving that you can be a nerd AND have a life. The secret? When you genuinely love what you're studying, those late-night coding sessions feel less like work and more like leveling up in a really hard video game.

What's it like juggling a startup, academic research, and still having time to exist?

Chaotic, caffeinated, and somehow fun? I'm currently working on my thesis, building Trimmly, doing client projects, and somehow enrolled in both the Young Leaders Programme and FSTEP Banking Program. People ask how I manage it all, and honestly? I just really hate being bored. Plus everything connects – my academic work teaches me to think systematically, my startup teaches me that theory and practice are very different things, and my client work keeps me grounded in reality. It's organized chaos, emphasis on organized... most days.

What car do you drive and why does it matter?

I drive a Tesla Model 3, which probably makes me sound like every other tech bro, but hear me out. It's less about the brand and more about making a bet on where things are heading. Electric vehicles aren't perfect, but they're better than pretending climate change isn't real. Plus, there's something satisfying about the tech – the software updates, the efficiency, the fact that it drives like a spaceship. It's basically me putting my money where my mouth is: if I'm building tech for the future, I might as well drive it too.

Full-stack developer, entrepreneur, eternal student - what's my philosophy on life?

Move fast, build cool stuff, and don't take yourself too seriously. I code in React, TypeScript, Node.js, and Python not because I'm trying to collect programming languages like Pokémon, but because the best tool depends on the problem. My 20s are basically my experimental phase – stack skills, take calculated risks, fail fast, learn faster. I'd rather try ten things and have seven fail spectacularly than play it safe and wonder "what if?" The worst outcome isn't failure, it's being boring enough that nobody remembers you tried.
